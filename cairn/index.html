00 — Adoption

Getting started

Adopt the system by declaring its tokens. CAIRN is tiered (--primitive → semantic → component) and themeable, so a designer and an engineer read from one source of truth. Set the active biome and everything retints.

1 · Load the three faces.   2 · Declare the token tiers on :root.   3 · Swap --biome per section, or data-theme="paper" on the root, to retint everything.

Tier 1 primitives are the only place a literal hex or pixel lives; semantic aliases (--surface, --accent, --action) map purpose to a primitive so a theme can swap underneath. See Production for the full architecture.

01 — Foundations

Six principles

The outdoors already has a design system, refined over a century. Each principle is lifted from something the outdoors does to keep you oriented, then written as a rule you can hold a design against.

01

Contours carry information

Lines describe the terrain, and their density is the data.

Topographic contours are the structural material. Tight lines read steep and intense, open lines calm and flat. The background is never decoration, it is a readable map of where weight and depth sit.

02

Blaze the way

One high-vis color marks where you are and where to go.

Blaze orange does a single job, the same job a painted trail blaze does on a tree: “you are here” and “this way.” Never spend it on decoration. When it appears, it means act, or look.

03

Color is terrain

Each section owns a biome, and the biome owns a hue.

Move into a new area and the field shifts: pine for forest, lichen for tundra, glacier blue for alpine, clay for desert. You learn the map by feel. Color does the wayfinding, not just the styling.

04

Stamped, sturdy type

Condensed signage faces, set like routed park signs.

Display type is upright, condensed, uppercase, the voice of a trail marker carved into wood. It holds up small, holds up huge, and reads at a glance from across the trailhead. Legible beats pretty.

05

Built to weather

High contrast, tactile materials, nothing fragile.

Warm near-black ground, kraft-canvas text, honest hairlines. The palette is earthy and the contrast is high so it survives glare, dust, and a cracked screen at altitude. Rugged is a usability requirement.

06

Set, do not float

Motion is grounded. Things settle into place with weight.

Where lighter languages drift and shimmer, this one lands. Elements arrive on a firm ease-out, like gear set down on rock. Nothing hovers or bounces. The interface feels solid underfoot.

The Unigrid

Vignelli’s 1977 system gave every National Park brochure one skeleton: a black band locks the title, a column grid holds the content, and a baseline keeps the rhythm. CAIRN inherits all three. Structure is a rule, not a suggestion.

10 — Guidance

Do, and do not

The discipline that keeps the language legible. Spend the blaze on one job, keep the terrain map honest, and let motion settle.

Do

  • Let topographic contours carry structure, and read their density as data.
  • Spend the blaze on one job: position and action, never decoration.
  • Recolor the whole field by biome so color does the wayfinding.
  • Anchor every layout to the band and the six-column grid.
  • Set display type condensed, upright, and uppercase, like a routed sign.
  • Keep contrast high and let motion settle with weight.

Do not

  • Treat contours as wallpaper, flat texture loses the information.
  • Scatter blaze orange around, a second “you are here” means none.
  • Mix biome hues inside one section, it breaks the terrain map.
  • Float content off the grid, the structure is the legibility.
  • Reach for delicate, low-contrast, hairline-thin type that fails in glare.
  • Add float, bounce, or shimmer, it breaks the grounded feel.
11 — Discipline

Legible in any condition

Rugged and accessible are the same goal here. A design that survives glare, gloves, and altitude is a design that survives low vision, motor limits, and a small screen. Built to weather means built for everyone.

Contrast

Body text holds 9:1 or better on the dark ground, and the blaze clears AA for UI. Nothing critical relies on color alone, every biome pairs with a label and an icon.

Targets

Tap targets meet 44px minimums so they work with gloves or a cold thumb. Focus is always visible with a blaze ring, and the order follows the trail.

Motion

Every animation is decorative and respects prefers-reduced-motion. The trail draws once, nothing loops in the reader’s face, nothing conveys meaning by movement alone.

Legibility

No hairline type, no low-contrast gray-on-gray. Minimum 1.6px strokes and a 14px floor on interface text, because anything thinner disappears in sun or on a scratched screen.

12 — Production

Ready for the field

A language is only mature when teams can ship it. CAIRN ships as tiered tokens, themeable surfaces, and components with an honest status, so a designer and an engineer read from one source of truth.

Token architecture
Tier 1

Primitive

Raw, context-free values. The only place a literal hex or pixel is allowed to live.

--bark: #15120c
--pine: #54905a
--blaze: #ff6a2b
--s4: 16px
Tier 2

Semantic

Intent, not appearance. Maps a purpose to a primitive so a theme can swap underneath it.

--surface: var(--soil)
--text: var(--canvas)
--accent: var(--biome)
--action: var(--blaze)
Tier 3

Component

What a part actually consumes. Reads tier 2, never a raw value, so nothing drifts.

--btn-bg: var(--action)
--card-pad: var(--s5)
--card-line: var(--border)
Implementation

13 — Heritage

Heritage

The outdoors already has a design system, refined over a century. CAIRN gathers that lineage — function first and weatherproof — and makes it buildable for a screen. Credited plainly.

1933
WPA Park Posters
Bold, flat, woodcut landscapes in earthy ink. The first deliberate visual identity for the American outdoors, cut by Depression-era artists.
1940s
USGS Topographic
Contour lines turn terrain into information. Brown contours on cream, density equals steepness. Pure function.
1977
Unigrid System
Massimo Vignelli’s grid unifies every National Park brochure. Bands, black bars, one structure. Content over decoration.
Now
Cairn
Contours, blazes, and biome color, re-tokenized into an interface you navigate by terrain.
A study in the open-air graphic tradition, descended from the functional graphic language of the outdoors: WPA park posters (1933), USGS topographic survey, Massimo Vignelli’s National Park Unigrid system (1977), and the trail blazes & stacked-stone cairns that are the most rugged wayfinding ever made.
Stack: Oswald · Asap · Space Mono · tiered CSS-variable tokens. Spirit: rugged, wayfinding, built to weather, function first.
