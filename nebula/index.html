Nebula

Translucent glass, bioluminescent green, and one beating core in the dark. A small, importable system of tokens and components — a respectful, modern homage to the original Xbox dashboard.

Get started Components

Getting started

Three lines to adopt the system. It's namespaced (--nb-* / .nb-*), ships no global reset, and styles no bare elements — so it drops into an existing project without collisions.

1 · Link the stylesheet and fonts.   2 · Add .nb-root to opt a region into the theme.   3 · Use tokens and components.

Want only the design tokens for your own components? You don't even need .nb-root — the --nb-* custom properties live on :root and are available everywhere. Tooling consumers can read nebula.tokens.json.

Elevation & motion

Shadows lift glass off the dark; the glow is a light source, not decoration. Motion drifts and breathes — it never snaps.

--nb-shadow-1
--nb-shadow-2
--nb-glow-md

Durations --nb-dur-fast 160ms · base 240ms · slow 480ms. Easings --nb-ease-organic (drift) and --nb-ease-glass (UI). The breathing core uses --nb-dur-pulse 5.5s.

Buttons

Four variants, three sizes, full state coverage. The primary carries the glow; everything else is glass or quiet.

Surfaces & cards

One frosted-glass material does most of the structural work — beveled, lit from above, translucent. .nb-glass is the material; .nb-card is the padded content block.

Forms

Inputs, labels, help text, and a switch built on a real checkbox for accessibility.

Badges & meters

Status tones map into the green world; feedback colors (warning amber, danger red, info teal) stay legible against the dark.

Lists & tabs

A glowing menu row with an active state, and a pill tab group.

Identity & controls

The signature: a living core, the X-nexus. Plus the controller-hint pill and keyboard key — small nods that make the homage legible without copying anything.

The nexusGlyph is a stylized, original mark — provide your own <svg><defs> as shown in the docs source. Do not reproduce the Xbox logo.

Theming

Every component reads from semantic tokens, so a theme is just a token override. Two ship in the box; rolling your own is a handful of lines.

Try it now with the now / 2001 toggle in the top bar — the entire page retints from one token change.

Motion patterns

The original booted into a black void where particles gathered into a core, flared, and resolved into a glowing X. Reserve this flash for one earned moment — an app launch, never routine UI. It's a documented pattern here, not a forced overlay.

Contained, skippable, and disabled under prefers-reduced-motion.

Accessibility

Maturity is mostly the unglamorous parts.

Built in

Consistent :focus-visible ring on every interactive component · prefers-reduced-motion disables breathing, reveals, and transitions · the switch is a real checkbox · accent-on-dark text meets WCAG AA at body sizes.

Your part

Lists and tabs are styled, not re-implemented — keep correct roles and aria-* · reserve the glow accent for emphasis and chrome, use --nb-text / --nb-text-dim for prose · don't place body text on the accent green.

Heritage

Where the look comes from, credited plainly.

2001
Original Xbox
Particles forming a liquid core, a glowing X, translucent menus. Concept by Seton Kim, produced at REZN8.
2001
The boot sound
Brian Schmidt synthesized it live on the audio chip — ~25 KB of ROM. "Power striving to break free."
2005–12
Frutiger Aero
Glass, water, and aurora everywhere. The optimistic era the original Xbox quietly predated.
Now
Nebula
The living-machine aesthetic, re-tokenized into a system you can ship with.
A design study, not affiliated with Microsoft or Xbox. Type, sound patterns, and the X-nexus mark are original evocations, not Microsoft assets.
With respect to the people behind the original screen: Seton Kim (concept, at REZN8) · Brian Schmidt (boot sound) · and the dashboard team — Victor Blanco, Sakphong Chanbai, Bradford Christian, Jim Helm — whose names hid in System Info for two decades.
