Getting started

Using it

Drop these custom properties into a stylesheet and you have the whole system: the brand palette, the gilded surface, and the rule / frame / inlay treatment. Add data-theme="lacquer" on <html> to switch the ivory invitation for black-lacquer-and-gold — try it from the top bar.

1 · Paste the :root tokens.   2 · Build with --frame + --inlay, never drop shadows.   3 · Gild the edges with --rule and concentrate jewel color into one focal point.   4 · Flip to Lacquer by setting data-theme.

Pair it with Memphis for the same decade's opposite instinct. Every hex and rule here is a starting point, not a mandate.

Surface

Frame & inlay

Gilt is flat. There are no drop shadows and no glow — depth comes from a thin gold hairline recessed just inside a black frame, and from chamfered corners cut off the rectangle, so panels read as lacquer inlaid with metal.

Motifs

The mark set

Seven recurring forms. Mirror them, stack them, and let symmetry carry the composition. Geometry stands in for nature — a sun becomes rays, a flower becomes a fan. Each demo's SVG is copy-ready.

Sunburst

The rising-sun fan of rays. The signature motif — use it for headers, corners, and grand entrances.

Chevron

Stacked arrowheads. Adds upward momentum and machine-age speed without breaking symmetry.

Ziggurat

The stepped skyscraper setback. Build hierarchy by stacking tiers, widest at the base.

The fan

The peacock / shell fan. A stylized bloom of nested arcs ribbed by straight lines.

Fluting

Parallel vertical lines, like a fluted column. Use as texture, a divider, or a ribbed backdrop.

Fountain

The cascade — arcs springing from a single point. The era's favorite way to draw water and light.

Inlaid frame

A bordered panel with chamfered corners and a centered lozenge. The deco way to dignify anything.

Principles

How to use it

Six rules that keep the system unmistakably deco — balance over scatter, line over fill, restraint over noise.

  • Symmetry is the spineMirror the composition around a center axis. Where Memphis scatters, Gilt aligns — balance reads as luxury here.
  • Gild the edgesDefine forms with thin gold and black rules, not fills. A 1px metallic hairline does more than a heavy border ever could.
  • Step and stackBuild hierarchy vertically with setbacks and tiers — ziggurat rhythm, widest at the base, tapering to a point.
  • Geometry over natureStylize the organic into rays, fans, and chevrons. A sun is a burst of lines; a flower is a folded fan.
  • Restraint, then opulenceKeep most of the page calm and empty, then concentrate the jewel color and gilding into one ornamental focal point.
  • Inlay, don't shadowSkip the drop shadow. Recess a gold hairline just inside a black frame, and chamfer the corners, so panels read as lacquer inlaid with metal.
Accessibility

Accessibility

A flat, high-contrast deco system has real advantages — and a few honest obligations. Both themes are tuned to clear WCAG AA for body text.

Built in

Near-black --text on warm --bg (and champagne-on-onyx in Lacquer) clears AA comfortably · a :focus-visible ring sits on every swatch, tab, and button · the drifting ornaments and rotating sunburst are decorative (aria-hidden) and freeze under prefers-reduced-motion · the theme toggle is real buttons.

Your part

Gold is luminous but light — reserve --gold for hairlines and edges, never body text on paper · set prose in --text / --text-soft · when you fill a panel with a jewel lacquer, switch text to --champagne or --paper and re-check contrast · don't rely on a badge's color alone — keep the word.

Origin

Heritage

It takes its name from the 1925 Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes in Paris — a declaration that modern luxury could be machine-made. Lacquer, chrome, exotic wood, and gold leaf, organized by ruler and compass.

1925
Paris Exposition
The Arts Décoratifs fair names the style: modern luxury, machine-made. Lacquer, chrome, exotic wood, and gold leaf, organized by ruler and compass.
1930
Machine age
It climbs the skyline — the Chrysler Building's steel sunburst, Rockefeller Center, the liner Normandie, Hollywood movie palaces, Cassandre's posters, and Gatsby's parties.
1937
Streamline Moderne
Geometry rounds into aerodynamic speed lines and horizontal banding — optimistic, expensive-looking, and unmistakably modern.
