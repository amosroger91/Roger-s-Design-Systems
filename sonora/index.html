Sonora

A loud, warm interface language of neon dusk and Memphis confetti, grounded in adobe and masa. Descended from Taco Bell's 1990s rebrand, when fast food went hot pink, purple, and a little bit jagged.

Getting started

Adopt the system by dropping in the fonts and the token layer. Everything is namespaced custom properties (--fuego, --sp-*, semantic --primary) plus simple .ui-* components — no global reset, so it sits beside your existing styles. Set data-theme to flip light/dark.

1 · Link the three faces — Bricolage Grotesque, DM Sans, Space Mono.   2 · Paste the token block.   3 · Build against the role tokens, set data-theme on any region.

Want only the loud brand layer for marketing surfaces? Use the five brand hues against --noche directly. For product UI, build against the semantic role tokens below — the same screen themes itself between light and dark.

Built for real product UI

Sonora runs at two volumes. The loud one sells; the quiet one ships. For dense, everyday screens the system dials the neon down to a single primary accent over warm neutrals, leans on semantic tokens and status colors, restrains the motion, and holds AA contrast — in light or dark. Same soul, lower volume.

The spine

Six principles

Each is lifted from something the 1990s brand actually did, then written as a rule you can hold a screen against.

01 · Ring the bell

One bold mark anchors everything. The bell is the gravity. Simple, friendly, unmistakable. Every layout earns its place by pointing back to that one icon.

02 · Neon dusk

Magenta to grape to turquoise — color as flavor. The palette is the sunset turned electric. Hot pink leads, purple deepens, teal cuts. Color is the taste of the brand, not decoration.

03 · Memphis confetti

Scatter geometry, but scatter it on purpose. Triangles, zigzags, dots, and bursts give the surface energy. Playful, never random. Confetti frames content and fills dead air with personality.

04 · Adobe ground

Terracotta and masa keep the neon human. Under the electric sky is warm clay and corn cream. The Southwestern base stops the brightness going cold. Warmth makes loud feel welcoming.

05 · Big friendly voice

Chunky type, lowercase warmth, honest prices. Headlines are heavy and a touch hand-made. Labels are lowercase and casual. Values stated plainly, sixty-cent-menu honest. Approachable beats polished.

06 · Pop, do not glide

Motion bounces, overshoots, lands with momentum. Where other languages slide or breathe, this one springs. Things scale past their mark and settle back, with forward energy — running for the border, not strolling there.

Do, and do not

Do

  • Let one bold bell anchor the composition and earn the eye.
  • Lead with hot magenta, deepen with grape, cut with turquoise.
  • Ground the neon in warm clay and masa so it stays human.
  • Scatter Memphis confetti with rhythm to fill dead air.
  • Set type heavy, lowercase, and friendly, with honest prices.
  • Make things pop and overshoot, with forward momentum.

Do not

  • Crowd the screen with competing marks; the bell loses its gravity.
  • Mute the palette into tasteful pastels; the loudness is the point.
  • Drop the warm ground; neon with no adobe goes cold and clinical.
  • Sprinkle confetti at random until it reads as noise, not energy.
  • Reach for thin, precious, all-caps type; it fights the friendliness.
  • Use slow, smooth, mechanical glides; they kill the bounce.
Accessibility

Accessibility

Loud neon needs care. A bright, saturated system has real energy — and a few honest obligations to stay legible for everyone.

Built in

The functional register holds WCAG AA at body sizes in both light and dark · status always reads through more than color — paired labels, dots, and icons · inputs carry a visible --ring focus halo · the drifting hero confetti and sky shift are decorative (aria-hidden) and freeze under prefers-reduced-motion, which disables every animation.

Your part

The neon hues are vivid but light — reserve full --fuego on --noche for display moments, and switch to the semantic role tokens for prose, where each value is tuned for contrast · never set body text directly on hot magenta or turquoise; pair them with --masa or --noche and re-check · keep the bell ring's sound optional and the burst purely visual.

Where it comes from

Heritage

Taco Bell opened the 1990s warm and dusty: a mission bell, adobe browns, an invitation to "make a run for the border." Then in 1995 it detonated. The new free-standing bell landed on a field of hot magenta, grape, and turquoise, wrapped in Memphis-Group geometry — zigzags, confetti dots, jagged bursts. Optimistic, loud, unmistakably of its moment. Sonora takes that arc, warm ground struck by electric neon, and makes it buildable.
