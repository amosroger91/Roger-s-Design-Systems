Design Systems

Six design languages, each a faithful, modern homage to a moment when software — or print — had an unmistakable point of view. Not screenshots: real tokens, components, and live documentation you can read, copy, and build with. Pick an era and press the buttons.

1925↗

Gilt

Art Deco · machine age

The 1925 Paris Exposition gone vertical — gilded geometry, jewel lacquer, and mirror symmetry from the Chrysler Building. Thin gold linework cut into shapes that mean business.

1930s–77↗

Cairn

Wayfinding

Rugged and weatherproof, descended from WPA park posters, USGS topo maps, and the NPS Unigrid. Color is terrain; one high-vis blaze cuts through any light.

…/cairn/live ↗
1991↗

Jazz

Pastel cup

The teal-and-purple squiggle off the 90s paper cup — soft pastels, scattered confetti, and rounded stickers. Pastel-postmodern, with a Soft/Loud switch.

…/jazz/live ↗
1995↗

Sonora

Neon dusk

The 1990s Taco Bell rebrand: warm Southwest ground — adobe and masa — detonated by electric neon and Memphis-Group confetti. Loud on purpose, grounded in the desert.

…/sonora/live ↗
2001↗

Nebula

Living machine

A respectful homage to the original Xbox dashboard — translucent glass, bioluminescent green, and one beating core in the dark. The full kit ships CSS + tokens.

…/nebula/live ↗
2001↗

Bliss

Glossy chrome

Friendly, optimistic chrome you can see and want to press — a homage to the Windows XP "Luna" style. Real component kit with three Luna schemes and a live desktop.

…/bliss/live ↗
2006↗

Aurora

Aero glass

Translucent, light-bending chrome and flowing color — a mature homage to Windows Vista and the Aero era of Frutiger optimism. Glass lit from within, not printed on.
